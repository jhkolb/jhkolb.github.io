I recently completed my PhD in computer science at the University of California, Berkeley. I am broadly interested in operating systems and distributed computing. I have also done research on applications of machine learning to education. I was part of the Building, Energy, and Transportation Systems (BETS) lab, a subsidiary of Berkeley's Rise Lab.

I received an M.S. in computer science at Berkeley for my work as part of the Software Defined Buildings project (the predecessor of BETS).

I received a B.S. in computer science from the University of Minnesota, where I was a member of the GroupLens research lab as well as the Distributed Computing Systems Group.
